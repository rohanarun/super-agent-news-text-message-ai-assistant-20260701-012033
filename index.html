What people mean by a “text message AI assistant”

Recent launches show growing interest in AI assistants that live inside messaging and SMS interfaces rather than standalone apps [theaijournal.com](https://theaijournal.com), [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com).

Most of these tools focus on conversation: answering questions, drafting replies, or summarising information — similar to how people use ChatGPT, Gemini, or Grok today.

Super takes a different approach: the text message is just the interface. Behind it is a real computer-using agent that can complete multi-step workflows and remember how it did them.

How Super turns texts into real actions

1. You send a text-style request

“Check my flight status and remind me if it changes.” You don’t open an app or configure an integration.

2. Super operates a computer

The agent opens websites, logs in where needed, and navigates like a human would — with your confirmation for sensitive steps.

3. Results + follow-ups

You get updates back in the same text thread. If this task repeats, Super can reuse its computer-use cache.

Why the computer-use cache matters

Unlike one-off chats, Super stores durable steps from previous runs. The computer-use cache means repeated workflows don’t start from zero every time.
